Nordisk Park

56° 26' 38.7168"

9° 25' 25.2912"

The Nordisk Park was landscaped in the period from 1994 to 1997 in an area at the east side of the "Søndersø".

The idea behind the park is that the five Nordic countries each have their own area, where plants, trees and landscape elements from the country in question must give associations to the country and remind the visitor of the community in the Nordics.

The landmark for Nordisk Park was created by the artist Jørgen Glud, who used the two towers of the cathedrals in Viborg and Lund as inspiration for the big N, which stands as a symbol of the Nordic thought. The two 8 meter long granite stones are from Sweden. They are connected by a huge piece of Norwegian pine timber held together by a ring of Finnish brass.
